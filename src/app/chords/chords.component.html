Chords on Bass

Where should we use chords on bass?

As bassists, our job is to outline the chord structure of the song. We often do so in a linear fashion (arpeggio), but should be able to hear and identify the chords... So perhaps we should learn some of those on bass.

This can be done with 10 shapes: 5 of them Open Voiced and 5 Closed Voice.

The 10 Shapes - Open and Closed Voice

"Closed Voiced" simply indicates that your fingering structure will remain within a single Octive... Or not ranging outside of the 14 notes that start with and include the Root note.

"Open Voiced" means that you will definately be landing a finger on a note that is outside of notes of the Root note's octive. You should assume you will be stretching over all 4 strings.

The Notes

'R' ... This indicates the Root note (will be 'C' if the chord is C (major|minor|dominant))

Roman Numerals ... These indicate the progression number (III is the 3rd, IV the 4th, V the 5th, etc)..

Flattened Roman Numerals ... Those which are flattened have a 'b' appending them (e.g. bIII is a flattened 3rd)
